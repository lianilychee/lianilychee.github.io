Hi! I'm Liani. Right now, I'm a robotics engineering junior at Olin College. I love rapid prototyping, especially in conjunction with direct-impact projects. My work last summer was a foray into the intersection between education and technology. I mentored three young ladies in middle-school from the Bronx to develop a scooter accessory for mobile device charging, patent pending.

I am currently discovering the joys of ROS (Robot Operating System), particularly in robot localization through particle filtering and ground truth localization with modified Neatos. Outside class on any given day, I can be found performing composite layups, characterizing sensors, or developing system architecture, all with the aim of building a sailboat to autonomously traverse the Atlantic Ocean.

Outside of engineering, I am working on increasing diversity at Olin, both through shifts in policy and by hosting Cultural Conversations. Cultural Conversations are student-body-wide chats that increase campus awareness and organic discussion around ethnic and cultural diversity both inside and outside the scope of the school.
