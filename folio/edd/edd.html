ENGINEERING DESIGN & DEVELOPMENT

Engineering Design & Development (EDD) is the California Academy of Math & Science capstone course that provides high-school seniors with true-to-life industry experience. The class, split into two independent but competing 16-member firms, is given ten months to produce from scratch a robot that fits the year's challenge. That year, we were tasked to develop a 6kg polymorphic robot with the capability to perform multiple tasks of value. Team performance is based on five critical design reviews throughout the year.

I had the privilege of serving as the Project Manager of one of the teams, Delta Technologies. We chose the military as the target audience because we wanted to prevent soldier casualties. As designers, we started by researching - talking to people in the military field. We discovered that real-time information is the most valuable asset on the field, so we structured our robot and tasks around improving military reconnaissance. Our goal of developing a robot capable of both land and air travel for better recon capabilities led us to investigate dibond, a light yet strong aluminum composite, as chassis material and we became the first team in EDD history to achieve stable flight.

As an entrepreneurial undertaking, we realized early-on that the scope we were striving to achieve would exceed the $1,500 budget. We were the first team in course history that worked to solicit outside donations from both for- and non-profit organizations, and we earned over $2,000, and roughly $1,000 of which consisted of donated printing and manufacturing services.

At the end of the year, the robot Mercury was able to succesfully perform the reconnaisance tasks we had set out to do: perform aerial room mapping, wirelessly transmit live video feed, detect a simulated mine, detect movement with discreet deployable modules.
