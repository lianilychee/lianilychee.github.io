Outreach Design: The Food Project Kiosk

The Food Project (TFP) is a Massachusetts organization that aims to make healthy, affordable produce accessible to working-class neighborhoods. In Anthropology: The Human Connection, five of my peers and I worked with a Tufts University graduate student to develop a mobile information kiosk for TFP.

The kiosk had five key characteristics:

  • Mobility. TFP is a low-staff organization. The kiosk needs to be easily transported and set up by a single person.

  • Versatility. The kiosk needed to be effective in multiple environments, from a sidewalk display to an indoor presentation aid.

  • Interactiveness

  • Durability

  • Presentability

The nature of the kiosk's function really forced me to think deeply about material selection and fabrication techniques. The work culminated in a scaled down sketch model.
