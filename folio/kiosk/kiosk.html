OUTREACH DESIGN: THE FOOD PROJECT KIOSK

In Anthropology: The Human Connection, five of my peers and I worked with a Tufts University graduate student to develop a mobile information kiosk for The Food Project. The Food Project (TFP) is a Massachusetts organization that aims to make healthy, affordable produce accessible to working-class neighborhoods.

This design project was my first in which the final deliverable was a mockup, rather than a prototype. Without the pressure of developing a flawless functional product, I found myself able to delve into the finer details - understanding the target audiences and their needs, as well as formulating use cases - that make up good design.

The final kiosk concept is based off the following key characteristics: mobility, versatility, interactiveness, durability, and presentatiblity. Each characteristic is addressed through thoughtful consideration of material used and fabrication techniques.
